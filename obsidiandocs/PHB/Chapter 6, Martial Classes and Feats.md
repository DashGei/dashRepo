## Hit Points

**Class Health Number:** 7

**Hit Points at 1st Level:** 14 + your fighting style health bonus

**Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 7 + your fighting style health bonus per level after 1st

## Proficiencies

- **Armor:** Light armor, medium armor, shields
- **Weapons:** Simple weapons, martial weapons
- **Saving Throws:** Strength, Charisma
- **Skills:** Choose two from Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, and Survival

## Physical and Mental Modifiers

- **Physical Modifier:** Strength modifier
- **Mental Modifier:** Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma modifier

## Fighting Style

You adopt a particular style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one of the following options. Each Fighting style grants you a bonus at level 1, you must be wielding one of your fighting style’s respective item to gain this benefit. At level 7, your fighting style provides you with a bonus.

- Dueling: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Polearm: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Sage: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Skirmisher: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Thrown: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Two-Weapon: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Unarmed: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Brawler: You gain a +3 health bonus at each level.
- Great Weapon: You gain a +3 health bonus at each level.
- Guardian: You gain a +3 health bonus at each level.

Each style grants a health bonus at every level. The bonus is added to your hit point maximum whenever you gain a level.

## Rage:1

You can imbue yourself with a primal power called Rage, a force that grants you extraordinary might and resilience.

### Entering Rage

You can enter your Rage by spending a **Feat Slot and an Action** if you aren't wearing Heavy armor.

### Active Rage

While your Rage is active, you gain the following benefits:

**Damage Resistance.** You have Resistance to Physical Damage.

**Rage Damage.** When you make an attack using Strength—with either a weapon or an Unarmed Strike—and deal damage to the target, you add additional dice, the number of dice at each level is shown on the Barbarian Features table.

**Strength Advantage.** You have Advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws.

**Critical Range.** Your attack rolls have a critical range. If the d20 rolls a number within that range, you score a critical hit. This range starts at 20 and increases in the following ways:

### Building Rage

Your rage now builds each turn, inceasing your offensive power.

- At the start of each of your turns while raging, your critical range increases by 1.
- Once per round, when an allied creature you can see within 10 feet of you takes damage, your critical range increases by 1.
- Your critical range cannot exceed a number lower than **20 - your Proficiency Bonus** (for example, at level 10 with a +4 Proficiency Bonus, the maximum range is 16–20).
- When you score a critical hit, your critical range immediately resets to 20.

### Duration

The Rage lasts **10 minutes**. It ends early if you are incapacitated or if you choose to end it as a **Free Action**.

### Unarmored Defense

**Unarmored Defense: 1.** While you are not wearing any armor, your Armor Class equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Strength modifier. Wearing a shield negates this effect.

## Danger Sense: 2

You gain an uncanny sense of when things nearby aren't as they should be, giving you an edge when you dodge away from danger.

You have advantage on Dexterity saving throws.

## Martial Feats: 2

**Feats Known.** You know 2 martial feats of your choice from your feat list. The Feats Known column of the Feats Progression table shows when you learn more feats. Additionally, when you gain a level in this class, you can replace one feat you know with another feat of your choice from your class feat list.

## Primal Path: 3

You choose a [Primal Path](sc_barbarian.html) that shapes the nature of your rage. Your choice grants you features at 3rd level, and then again at 7th, 14th, and 20th level.

## Primal Knowledge: 3

You gain proficiency in another skill of your choice from the skill list available to Barbarians at 1st level.

In addition, while your Rage is active, you can channel primal power when you attempt certain tasks: whenever you make an ability check using one of the following skills, you can make it as a Strength check even if it normally uses a different ability: Acrobatics, Intimidation, Perception, Stealth, or Survival. When you use this ability, your Strength represents primal power coursing through you, honing your agility, bearing, and senses.

## Ability Score Improvement

At 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 1. You can't increase an ability score above 5 using this feature.

## Martial Fatigue Resistance: 5

When taking the attack action you do not suffer any penalties from the fatigued condition.

### Rage Action

When you take the **Attack** action while raging, you can choose one of the following benefits to apply to your melee attacks this turn:

**Focused Fury.** You gain advantage on all melee attack rolls this turn, but attack rolls against you have advantage until the start of your next turn.

**Reckless Abandon.** When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can add a **d4** to the damage roll, but attack rolls against you have advantage until the start of your next turn.

## Fast Movement: 6

Your speed increases by 10 feet while you aren't wearing heavy armor.

## Brutal Critical I: 9

Beginning at 9th level, the number of dice you add with Reckless Abandon increases by 1, and the dice size increases to a d6

## Insurmountable: 10

When you are hit with an attack that also applies an effect, whether it requires a saving throw or not, you may spend a Feat Slot to make a Strength saving throw. The DC equals 10 or half the damage you take, whichever number is higher. If you succeed, you take damage as normal, but you do not suffer the effect.

## Relentless Rage: 11

Your rage can keep you fighting despite grievous wounds. If you drop to 0 hit points while you're raging and don't die outright, you can make a DC 10 Strength saving throw. If you succeed, you drop to 1 hit point instead.

Each time you use this feature after the first, the DC increases by 5. When you finish a short or long rest, the DC resets to 10.

## Brutal Critical II: 13

Beginning at 13th level, the number of dice you add with Focused Fury increases by 1, and the dice size increases to a d8

## For the Fences: 15

When rolling with disadvantage, if you roll a number within your critical hit range, you can use that roll instead of the lowest of the two d20.

## Brutal Critical III: 17

Beginning at 17th level, the number of dice you add with Focused Fury increases by 1, and the dice size increases to a d10

## Impeccable Guardian: 17

Also at 17th level you gain the following bonuses:

- Your speed increases by an additional 15 feet while you aren't wearing heavy armor.
- When a creature within 30 feet of you makes a strength saving throw, you can use your reaction to move into their space and treat yourself as the target instead. The creature you swapped with is moved up to 5 feet to an additional space of their choosing

## Indomitable Might: 18

If your total for a Strength check is less than your Strength Modifier × 3, you can use that score in place of the total.

As an action, choose a creature you can see within 30 feet. Expend one Hemo Die and roll it. You take damage equal to the number rolled (this damage cannot be reduced or prevented in any way), and the target is Doomed for 10 minutes.

The next time a creature deals damage to the Doomed target, one Hemo Die is added to the damage roll (the attacker rolls an additional Hemo Die and adds it to the damage). The attacker and you each regain hit points equal to the extra damage dealt. If the damage was dealt by you, you regain hit points only once. The Doom then ends on that target.

## Ability Score Improvement

At 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 1. You can't increase an ability score above 5 using this feature.

## Pain Bank: 4

As an action, you can spend one Feat Slot to enter a Pain Bank. Determine your Fury magnitude based on your current hit points:

- 1 if you are at or below three‑quarters of your hit point maximum.

2 if you are at or below half your hit point maximum.

3 if you are at or below one‑quarter of your hit point maximum. When you enter the surge, you gain temporary hit points equal to the total rolled on a number of Hemo Dice equal to your Fury magnitude + your Charisma modifier (minimum 1 die).

- While you have these temporary hit points, the following effects apply:
- Bleeding Shield. At the start of each of your turns, you lose temporary hit points equal to one roll of your Hemo Die. This loss cannot be reduced or prevented. If this reduces your temporary hit points to 0, the Pain Bank ends immediately.
- Sanguine Strikes. Once per turn when you hit a creature with a melee attack, you deal extra necrotic damage equal to one roll of your Hemo Die. You regain hit points equal to the necrotic damage dealt. This healing cannot exceed your hit point maximum.

The Pain Bank ends early if you are incapacitated or if you choose to end it (no action required).

## Martial Fatigue Resistance: 5

When taking the attack action you do not suffer any penalties from the fatigued condition.

## Shadowfell Magicks: 6

If you consume a drop of a creature’s blood, you forge a temporary supernatural bond. The creature makes a Physical DC Charisma save. If they fail, then for 1 hour, you know the cardinal direction to that creature, and you know when it is lying to you. After the hour ends, that creature becomes immune to this effect until you finish a long rest.

## Crimson Chain: 6

Once per turn, when an allied creature you can see within 10 feet of you takes damage, you can regain one expended Hemo Die.

## Hand of Doom: 7

A creature effected by Pact of Doom takes 1d4 Necrotic damage when they take the attack action against a creature that is not you.

Once per turn, when you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can apply your Pact of Doom to that target as part of the same attack (no additional action required). You still expend a Hemo Die and take the damage as normal.

## Deepend Bond: 9

Your Hemo Die becomes a d8. In addition, when you roll a Hemo Die for self‑damage from your class features, you can roll it twice and take the lower or higher result.

## Comfortably Numb: 10

When you start your turn with temporary hit points from your Pain Bank, you can choose to gain resistance to all damage except psychic and radiant until the start of your next turn. If you do, the Bleeding Shield loss at the start of your turn is doubled (roll two Hemo Dice and lose that many temporary hit points). You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

## Shared Vitality: 11

Additionally, whenever you roll your Hemo Die, you may spend an additional die to roll another Hemo Die and add it to the roll.

## Double the Pain: 13

When you use Pact of Doom, you can expend two Hemo Dice instead of one. If you do, you take necrotic damage equal to the sum of both dice, and the bonus damage added to the next attack against the Doomed creature equals the sum of both dice. Both the attacker and you regain hit points equal to this total.

## Font of Vitality: 15

When you enter your Pain Bank all creatures of your choice within 30 feet of you regain hit points equal to one roll of your Hemo Die.

## Deepend Bond II: 17

Your Hemo Die becomes a d10.

## Die Another Day: 18

As a reaction when an allied creature is reduced to 0 hitpoints, you can flood their body with necromantic energy and revive them. The target immediately regains 1 hit point, then gains the following benefits as if they had entered a Pain Bank using your Hemo Dice and Charisma modifier: Bleeding Shield. They gain temporary hit points equal to the total rolled on a number of your Hemo Dice equal to your Mental Modifier (minimum 1). At the start of each of their turns, they lose temporary hit points equal to one roll of your Hemo Die. This loss cannot be reduced or prevented. If this reduces their temporary hit points to 0, the effect ends immediately. Sanguine Strikes. Once per turn when they hit a creature with a melee attack, they deal extra necrotic damage equal to one roll of your Hemo Die. They regain hit points equal to the necrotic damage dealt. This healing cannot exceed their hit point maximum. The effect also ends early if they are incapacitated. Once you use this feature, you must finish a long rest before you can use it again.

## Hit Points

**Class Health Number:** 6

**Hit Points at 1st Level:** 12 + your fighting style health bonus

**Hit Points at Higher Levels:** 6 + your fighting style health bonus per ranger level after 1st

## Proficiencies

**Armor:** Light armor, medium armor, shields

**Weapons:** Simple weapons, martial weapons

**Tools:** None

**Saving Throws:** Strength, Dexterity

**Skills:** Choose three from Animal Handling, Athletics, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Stealth, and Survival

## Physical and Mental Modifiers

Some of your abilities will require your Mental or Physical Modifier.

Your Physical Modifier is your Strength or Dexterity Modifier.

Your Mental Modifier is your Wisdom Modifier.

## Fighting Style: 1

You adopt a particular style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one of the following options. Each Fighting style grants you a bonus at level 1, you must be wielding one of your fighting style’s respective item to gain this benefit. At level 7, your fighting style provides you with a Martial Feat, this feat does not count against your Feats known, and it does not require a slot when you use it. You must be wielding one of your fighting style’s respective item to use this feature, but you can use this feat regardless of your class.

- Archery: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Dueling: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.

- Thrown Weapon: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Unarmed: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Two-Weapon: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Sage: You gain a +2 health bonus at each level.
- Guardian: You gain a +3 health bonus at each level.
- Great Weapon: You gain a +3 health bonus at each level.
- Druidic Warrior: You gain a +3 health bonus at each level.

## Hunter's Mark: 1st-Level Ranger Feature

Beginning at 1st level, with an action you designate an enemy you can see within 90 feet of you as your chosen enemy. While this enemy is marked you have advantage on any Wisdom (Perception or Survival) checks you make to find or track it. Whenever you hit a marked creature with an attack you may roll a number of d2s equal to your proficiency bonus and add them to the damage total.

The mark lasts until you next take a long rest, until the creature dies, or you spend a Feat Slot as a free action to remove the mark. The creature is marked A creature has no way of knowing that it has been marked

## Aspect Bond: 1

You have learned to bond with the natural creatures of the world, channeling their traits to enhance your own abilities. You gain access to animal aspects, which are grouped into tiers representing their power and complexity.

**Aspects Known.** You know 3 aspects of your choice from the Tier 1 aspect list. You learn additional aspects as shown in the Aspects Known column of the Ranger Features table. When you gain a level in this class, you can replace one aspect you know with another aspect of the same tier.

**Aspect Tiers.** Aspects are organized into three tiers of power. You gain access to higher tiers at levels 1, 8, and 14, as shown in the Aspect Tier column.

**Preparing Aspects.** You can have 2 aspects active simultaneously During a short or long rest, you can meditate and commune with nature to swap out any or all of your known aspects for other aspects you know.

**Aspect Categories.** Aspects are grouped into categories based on the type of benefit they provide. You cannot have more than acone tive aspect from the same category at one time. The categories are: Offense, Proficiency, Movement, Resistance, Save Resistance, and Misc.

## Martial Feats: 2

You have trained in special techniques that give you an edge in combat and beyond. You gain access to martial feats, as described in the Martial Feats section. You may only use one Feat Slot per turn.

**Primary Slots.** You have 2 Primary Slots, which you expend to activate your feats. You regain all expended slots when you finish a short rest. Your number of Primary Slots increases as you gain levels, as shown in the Feats Progression table.

**Feats Known.** You know 2 martial feats of your choice from your feat list. The Feats Known column of the Feats Progression table shows when you learn more feats. Additionally, when you gain a level in this class, you can replace one feat you know with another feat of your choice from your class feat list.

**Feat Tier.** You can learn feats of Tier 1 at level 2. At higher levels you gain access to higher tiers, as shown in the table.

## Natural Explorer: 2

You are a master of navigating the natural world. You gain the following benefits:

- Once per long rest you may use a Feat with the Exploration tag without expending a Feat Slot.
- You and your group can't become lost except by magical means.
- Even when you are engaged in another activity while traveling (such as foraging, navigating, or tracking), you remain alert to danger.
- When you forage, you find twice as much food as normal.
- While tracking other creatures, you also learn their exact number, their sizes, and how long ago they passed through the area.

## Ranger Conclave: 3

You choose a [Ranger Conclave](sc_ranger.html) that embodies your hunting philosophy. Your choice grants you features at 3rd level and then again at 7th, 10th, and 15th level.

You've dealt with the dangers of this world for quite some time now, and you know . When you reduce a creature to 0 hit points with a weapon attack, you may immediately apply hunter’s mark to a new creature without the use of an action.

## Aspect Expansion: 8

You can choose up to two aspects of the same category.

## Nature's Veil: 10

You can draw on the aspects of creatures that blend into their surroundings. As an action, you can become invisible until the end of your next turn. While you are invisible in this way, a creature effected by Hunter's Mark has disadvantage on Intitiative rolls.

This feature requires the use of a Feat Slot, when you use this feature, mark one Feat Slot.

## Analyzed II: 13

When you hit a marked creature with an attack, instead of dealing extra damage from your Hunter's Mark feature, you can instead choose to remove one damage resistance it has until the start of your next turn.

Additionally, once per turn when you miss a marked creature with an attack roll you may make another with disadvantage. This roll always has disadvantage even if it would be neutralized by a source of advantage

## Traversal Master: 15

You learn all feats with the Exploration tag, and you no longer have to expend feat slots to use them.

## Evasion: 17

When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, and only half damage if you fail.

## Feral Senses: 18

You gain preternatural senses that help you fight creatures you can't see. You have Blindsight, you see invisible creatures and objects as if they were visible, and you can see into the Ethereal Plane out to 30 feet.

## Sneak Attack: 1

You know how to strike subtly and exploit a foe's distraction. Once per turn, you can deal an extra 1d6 damage to one creature you hit with an attack if you have advantage on the attack roll. The attack must use a finesse or ranged weapon.

You don't need advantage on the attack roll if another enemy of the target is within 5 feet of it, that enemy isn't incapacitated, and you don't have disadvantage on the attack roll.

The amount of the extra damage increases as you gain scoundrel levels, as shown in the Sneak Attack column of the scoundrel table. The extra damage's type is the same as the weapon's type.

## Thieves' Cant: 1

During your training, you learned thieves' cant, a secret mix of dialect, jargon, and code that allows you to hide messages in seemingly normal conversation. Only another creature that knows thieves' cant understands such messages. It takes four times longer to convey such a message than it does to speak the same idea plainly.

In addition, you understand a set of secret signs and symbols used to convey short, simple messages.

## Jack of All Trades: 2

You can add half your proficiency bonus, rounded down, to any ability check you make that doesn't already include your proficiency bonus.

Additionally, choose two of your skill proficiencies to gain expertise in.

## Opportunistic Archetype: 3

At 3rd level, you choose an Opportunistic Archetype that emulates how you exploit your enemies. Your [choice](sc_scoundrel.html) grants you features at 3rd level, and then again at 7th, 14th, and 20th level.

## Quick Feet: 3

At 3rd level, your agility in pursuit is unmatched. When you take the Dash action, you gain an additional 15 feet of movement for that turn.

## Ability Score Improvement

At 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 1. You can't increase an ability score above 5 using this feature.

## Uncanny Dodge: 5

At 5th level, when an attacker that you can see hits you with an attack, you can use your reaction to halve the attack's damage against you.

## Expertise: 6

At 6th level, choose two of your skill proficiencies, or one of your skill proficiencies and your proficiency with thieves' tools. Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check you make that uses either of the chosen proficiencies.

## Evasion: 9

At 9th level, when you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, and only half damage if you fail.

## Opportunistic Strike

At 10th level, you have an eye for capitalizing on even the smallest mistakes. When a creature you can see within 30 feet of you rolls a natural 1 on an attack roll, you can use your reaction and expend a Feat Slot to activate this feature. Choose one of the following effects:

Move up to half your speed toward that creature and make one melee weapon attack against it.

Make one ranged weapon attack against that creature.

This attack is automatically a Sneak Attack, dealing your full Sneak Attack damage dice even if you wouldn't normally qualify for it. You do not need advantage, nor does an ally need to be within 5 feet of the target.

## Reliable Talent

At 11th level, you have refined your chosen skills until they approach perfection. Whenever you make an ability check that lets you add your proficiency bonus, if the roll doesn't have advantage or disadvantage, roll two d20s and use whichever roll is closer to 11. If there is a tie (for example, 9 and 13), you take the higher roll.

## Skill Specialization

At 13th level, you hone your skills to an exceptional degree. Choose one skill or tool proficiency that you are proficient in. Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check you make that uses that skill or tool. This stacks with Expertise (you can choose a skill you already have Expertise in, but the bonus does not double again; instead you gain advantage on checks with that skill).

Alternatively, you can choose to gain proficiency in two additional skills of your choice.

## Blindsense

At 15th level, if you are able to hear, you are aware of the location of any hidden or invisible creature within 10 feet of you.

## Dirty Tactics

Also at 15th level, whenever you roll with advantage you roll 3 d20 and take the highest roll.

## Slippery Mind

At 17th level, your cunning mind is difficult to assault. You gain proficiency in Wisdom saving throws.

## Elusive

At 18th level, you are so evasive that attackers rarely gain the upper hand against you. No attack roll has advantage against you while you aren't incapacitated.

## Stroke of Luck

At 20th level, you have an uncanny knack for succeeding when you need to most. If you fail an ability check or attack roll, you can choose to succeed instead. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

## Opportunistic Archetype Capstone

At 20th level, you gain the ultimate expression of your chosen Opportunistic  Archetype.

# Martial Feats

Martial Feats represent specialized techniques, combat maneuvers, and trained abilities that martial characters can perform in battle.

Unlike spells, Martial Feats are fueled by @Feat Slots. You expend Feat Slots to activate powerful abilities and regain all expended Feat Slots when you finish a @Short Rest.

Martial Feats are divided into four tiers. As you gain levels, you unlock access to more powerful feats and gain additional Feat Slots to fuel them.

---

## Feat Slots

Feat Slots represent your endurance, training, and ability to perform exceptional techniques under pressure.

When you activate a feat that requires a Feat Slot, you expend one of your available slots. You regain all expended Feat Slots when you finish a Short Rest.

## Learning Feats

As you gain levels, you learn Martial Feats from your class feat list.

Each feat belongs to one of four tiers:

|Tier|Level Required|
|---|---|
|Tier 1|2nd|
|Tier 2|5th|
|Tier 3|11th|
|Tier 4|17th|

When you learn a new feat, you may choose any feat from a tier you have access to.

Some class features, subclasses, and @Fighting Styles grant additional feats. Unless stated otherwise, these feats do not count against your Feats Prepared.

---

## Using a Feat

Each feat describes when and how it can be activated.

A feat may require an action, a reaction, an attack, or another trigger. If a feat does not specify an action cost, activating it requires no action.

Unless a feat states otherwise, activating a feat costs one Feat Slot.

### One Feat Per Turn

You cannot activate more than one feat during your turn.

Feats activated as reactions on your own or another creature's turn do not count toward this limit.

### Feat Saving Throws

Some feats require a target to make a saving throw.

When a feat calls for a saving throw, it uses either your @Physical DC or your @Mental DC, as specified in the feat's description.

A feat that lists:

**Physical DC Strength**

means the target makes a Strength saving throw against your Physical DC.

A feat that lists:

**Mental DC Wisdom**

means the target makes a Wisdom saving throw against your Mental DC.
