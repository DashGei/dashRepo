**Sometimes** a session might have or call for an extended period of time between social or combat encounters during this period characters have time to explore the world, for connections, and work on their equipment.

This chapter also covers the powers and mechanics the entire party gain access to at certain levels once they become fully fledged adventurers.

# Leveling up

When the party accomplishes a great task, or possibly before a major plot point, the DM lets the party level up (usually the next time they take a long rest).


# Level 2, Crafting Equipment

Using the rules below, characters can make nonmagical items, Potions of Healing, and Spell Scrolls.

## Crafting Nonmagical Items

To craft a nonmagical item, you need **tools**, **raw materials**, and **time**, each of which is detailed below.

If you meet the requirements, you make the item, and you can use it or sell it at its normal price.

### Tools

This chapter's **Tools** section lists which tools are required to make certain items. The DM assigns required tools for items not listed there. Some tools, especially those which require metal, might require a workstation such as a forge determined by the DM.

You must use the required tool to make an item and have proficiency with that tool. Anyone who helps you must also have proficiency with it.

### Raw Materials

To make an item, you need raw materials worth **half its purchase cost** (round down).

For example, you need **750 GP** of raw materials to make **Plate Armor**, which sells for **1,500 GP**.

The DM determines whether appropriate raw materials are available.

### Time

To determine how many days (**working 8 hours a day**) it takes to make an item, divide its purchase cost in GP by **10** (round a fraction up to a day).

For example, you need **5 days** to make a **Crossbow**, which sells for **50 GP**.

If an item requires multiple days, the days needn't be consecutive.

Characters can combine their efforts to shorten the crafting time. Divide the time needed to create an item by the number of characters working on it.

Normally, only one other character can assist you, but the DM might allow more assistants.

## Brewing Potions of Healing

A character who has proficiency with the **Herbalism Kit** can create a **Potion of Healing**.

Doing so requires:

- An Herbalism Kit
- **25 GP** of raw materials
- **1 day** (8 hours of work)

Upon completion, you create one **Potion of Healing**.

# Scribing Spell Scrolls

A spellcaster can transfer a spell to a scroll and create a **Spell Scroll**, using the rules below.

## Time and Cost

Scribing a scroll takes an amount of time and money based on the level of the spell, as shown in the **Spell Scroll Costs** table.

For each day of inscription, you must work for **8 hours**. If a scroll requires multiple days, those days needn't be consecutive.

## Prerequisites for the Scribe

To scribe a scroll, you must:

- Have proficiency in the **Arcana** skill and with **Calligrapher's Supplies**
- Have the spell prepared on each day of the inscription
- Have at hand any Material components required by the spell

If the spell consumes its Material components, they are consumed only when you complete the scroll.

The scroll's spell uses your **spell save DC** and **spell attack bonus**.

## Cantrips

If the scribed spell is a cantrip, the version on the scroll works as if the caster were your level.

## Level 4, Mentorship

When the party reaches level 4 , they are able to share with each other their skills and knowledge. During a long rest a party member (The Mentor) can impart a portion of their learned skills to another party member (The Student).

### Mentorship Process

1. **Initiation.** At the start of a long rest, the Mentor and Student declare they are spending the rest in focused training.
    
2. **Teaching.** The Mentor selects one proficiency or minor ability they possess, from the list below, to impart. The Student must lack that proficiency or ability.
    
3. **Rest Completion.** At the end of the long rest, the Student makes an ability check using the Mentoring ability.
    
    - **Success:** The Student gains the taught feature immediately. See below the table for what each lesson gives you.
        
    - **Failure:** The Student retains the partial training. They gain advantage on the next attempt to learn this same feature from the same Mentor. If they fail three times, the lesson is beyond them for now, and they cannot attempt to learn that specific feature from that Mentor again until they gain a level.

### What Can Be Taught?

| Category               | Examples                                      | Mentoring Ability                               | Mentoring DC |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------ |
| **Language**           | One language the Mentor speaks                | Intelligence                                    | 21           |
| **Tool Proficiency**   | One tool kit, vehicle, or gaming set          | Dexterity or Intelligence (appropriate to tool) | 18           |
| **Skill Proficiency**  | One skill the Mentor is proficient in         | The skill’s associated ability                  | 20           |
| **Cantrip**            | One cantrip the Mentor knows                  | The Mentor’s Mental Modifier                    | 21           |
| **Weapon Proficiency** | One weapon the Mentor is proficient in        | Dexterity or Strength                           | 18           |
| **Martial Feat**       | One Martial Feat from the Mentor's class list | The Mentor’s Intelligence or Wisdom Modifier    | 20           |
**Language**: The student adds the language to their languages known.

**Tool**: The student has proficiency in the tool.

**Skill**: The student has proficiency in that skill.

**Cantrip**: That student learns the cantrip, using their Mental Modifier for attack rolls and spell save DC.

**Weapon Proficiency**: The student becomes proficient in the weapon.

**Martial Feat**: The student adds the learned Feat to their class's Feats known.

**Restrictions.**

- A Mentor can teach only one Student per long rest.
    
- A Student can learn from only one Mentor per long rest.
    
- A Student can learn at most **two** features through Mentorship. Once they have learned two, they can still serve as a Mentor, but cannot be a Student again.
    
- You cannot teach a feature that is granted by a class, feature, or background that you only have access to temporarily (e.g., from a spell effect or ranger aspect).


## Level 6, Divine Favor

**For many in the world of Gaia**, Religion plays a communal and utilitarian role in one's life. Rarely will individuals devote themselves to singular gods, or swear all of them off outright.

A typical peasant may pray to Gri in the morning for a safe crop, evoke Ilxi's name while gambling with their friends, and then go home where an emblem of Helm sits above the door and guards against evils.

### Player Interactions

Just like everyone else, players can form relationships with the various gods of the world, gaining boons, or possibly minor woes if they repeatedly offend some deities.

Not every god preferers the same method of worship. Some gods wish only for larger and larger masses of dedicated worshippers some simply want their follower's to practice their disciplines, and others care solely that their ideals are spread. 

### Divine Favor

The party loses or gains **Favor Points**  by supporting a deity's efforts and ideals as detailed in each deity's Favor Criteria section, at the DM's discretion unmentioned actions that the party takes may effect relationships with the gods.

Favor grants the entire party small, thematic blessings. Acting against a god’s principles can bring minor woes. A party who makes meaningful connections with each other and the world will always bring favor.

When the party finishes a long rest together, they may ask the DM to reveal their current standings with the gods. They may also choose to voluntarily decrease their Favor with any deity by any amount at that time to make room for other relationships.

### Favor Scale

A party’s standing with a deity ranges from -5 to +10.

| Favor Score | Status     | Effect      |
| ----------- | ---------- | ----------- |
| -5          | Disfavored | Greater Woe |
| -4 to -3    | Displeased | Minor Woe   |
| 0           | Neutral    | ---         |
| 1 - 2       | Noticed    | ---         |
| 3 - 5       | Favored    | Minor Boon  |
| 6 - 9       | Honored    | Boon        |
| 10          | Champion   | Major Boon  |

Favor Points adjust at the end of a session at the DM’s discretion. The party cannot lose or gain more than 5 FP per session with a given god through direct offense, and minor slights may not change the score at all.


The party can hold a maximum of 30 Favor Points invested across a maximum of 5  deities at any time. Negative Favor Points do not add to this total, and the party can have a maximum of 10 total negative Favor Points invested. If the party would gain FP that would push their total above 30, those points are lost.

### Worship Methods

Every deity has a **Worship Method** that dictates how the party gains Favor. The DM awards 1 FP when the party meaningfully fulfills the method, and occasionally 2 FP for exceptional acts.

| Method         | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Devotion**   | The god grows stronger through worship. FP is earned by spreading the faith, building shrines, converting NPCs, or leading communal prayers.                                                                            |
| **Discipline** | The god values the practice of a craft, skill, or way of life. FP is earned by engaging in the associated discipline. Creating art, mastering a trade, or upholding a code may be the best way to earn this gods favor. |
| **Ideals**     | The god cares that their ideals are enacted. FP is earned by championing those ideals, defeating opposing forces, or shifting a community’s values.                                                                     |

### Boons & Woes

Boons are occasional minor bonuses that the party may obtain by maintaining good relationships with gods. Each god has their own set of boons, some may give players resources to spend during the day, some may only activate when the DM chooses.





# Marching Order

When traveling through dangerous areas, the party should establish a marching order.

The marching order determines:

- Who encounters danger first.
    
- Who can see approaching threats.
    
- Where characters begin combat.


Characters in the front are more likely to encounter hazards, while characters in the rear may be vulnerable to ambushes.
