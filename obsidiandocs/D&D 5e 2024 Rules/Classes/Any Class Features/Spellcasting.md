#### Spellcasting
You have learned to cast spells through means determined by your class. See [[Spells]] for the rules on spellcasting. The information below details how you use those rules with your class's spells, which appear in that class's spell list at the end of the class's description. I have also included all of the spell lists at the end of the spellcasting rules.

***Cantrips (Artificer, Bard, Cleric, Druid, Sorcerer, and Wizard Only).*** You know two cantrips of your choice from the Artificer, Bard, or Druid spell lists, three cantrips of your choice from the Cleric or Wizard spell lists, or four cantrips of your choice from the Sorcerer spell list depending on what class you obtained this feature from. 

Regardless of which class you obtained this feature from, when you gain a level in that class, you can replace one of your cantrips with another cantrip of your choice from that class's spell list. If you are an Artificer, you can also do this whenever you finish a [[Long Rest]]

When you reach levels 4 and 10 in a class that grants you this feature, you learn another cantrip of your choice from that class's spell list. Artificers instead get their additional cantrips at levels 10 and 14. This is shown on the Cantrips columns of each Class Features table.

***Spell Slots.*** The Class Features tables show how many spell slots you have to cast your level 1+ spells. You regain all expended slots when you finish a Long Rest.

***Prepared Spells of Level 1+.*** You prepare the list of level 1+ spells that are available for you to cast with this feature. To start, choose a number of level 1 spells from your class's spell list equal to the number in the Prepared Spells column of the Class Features table at the level you obtained this feature.

The number of spells on your list increases as you gain class levels, as shown in that column. The chosen spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots. If another class feature gives you spells that you always have prepared, those spells don't count against the number of spells you can prepare with this feature, but those spells otherwise count as class spells for you.

***Changing Your Prepared Spells.*** Whenever you gain a Bard or Sorcerer level, you can replace one spell on your list with another spell from that class's list for which you have spell slots. Whenever you finish a Long Rest, you can change your list of prepared Artificer, Cleric, Druid, and Wizard spells, replacing any of the spells with other spells from that class's list for which you have spell slots if you're an Artificer, Cleric, or Druid or from your spellbook if you're a Wizard. You can also replace one spell on your Paladin or Ranger list with another spell from that class's list for which you have spell slots.

***Spellcasting Ability.*** Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for your Artificer and Wizard spells. Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for your Cleric, Druid, and Ranger spells. Charisma is your spellcasting ability for your Bard, Paladin, and Sorcerer spells.

***Spellcasting Focus.*** You can use a [[Musical Instruments|Musical Instrument]] as a Spellcasting Focus for your Bard spells, a [[Holy Symbol]] as a Spellcasting Focus for your Cleric and Paladin spells, a [[Druidic Focus]] as a Spellcasting Focus for your Druid and Ranger spells, or an [[Arcane Focus]] as a Spellcasting Focus for your Sorcerer or Wizard spells. You can also use your [[spellbook]] as a Spellcasting Focus for your Wizard spells.

You produce your Artificer spells through tools. You can use [[Thieves' Tools]], [[Tinker's Tools]], or another kind of Artisan's Tools with which you have proficiency as a Spellcasting Focus, and you must have one of those focuses in hand when you cast an Artificer spell (meaning the spell has an *M* [[Components|component]] when you cast it).

***Spellbook (Wizard Only).*** Your wizardly apprenticeship culminated in the creation of a unique book: your spellbook. It is a Tiny object that weighs 3 pounds, contains 100 pages, and can be read only by you or someone casting Identify. You determine the book’s appearance and materials, such as a gilt-edged tome or a collection of vellum bound with twine.

The book contains the level 1+ spells you know. It starts with six level 1 Wizard spells of your choice. Detect Magic, Feather Fall, Mage Armor, Magic Missile, Sleep, and Thunderwave are recommended. Whenever you gain a Wizard level after 1, add two Wizard spells of your choice to your spellbook. Each of these spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots, as shown in the Wizard Features table. The spells are the culmination of arcane research you do regularly.


Spellcasting. Note both the saving throw DC for your spells and the attack bonus for attacks you make with them, using these formulas:

Spell save DC = 8 + Mental Modifier + Proficiency Bonus

Spell attack bonus = Mental Modifier + Proficiency Bonus

Your spellcasting ability modifier for a spell is determined by whatever feature gives you the ability to cast the spell.

Spell Slots, Cantrips, and Prepared Spells. If your class gives you the Spellcasting or Pact Magic feature, your class features table shows the number of spell slots you have available, how many cantrips you know, and how many spells you can prepare. Choose your cantrips and prepared spells, and note them—along with your number of spell slots—on your character sheet.

### Spell Slot Tiers

Spell slots are divided into three tiers: Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary. Each tier corresponds to a range of spell levels.

- Primary Slots: The most abundant, used for lower‑level spells.
    
- Secondary Slots: Fewer in number, used for mid‑level spells.
    
- Tertiary Slots: Rare, used for the most powerful spells a caster can wield.
    

The progression tables for full casters and half‑casters are found in each class’s description. For example, a 5th‑level full caster has 4 primary slots (1st level), 3 secondary slots (2nd level), and 1 tertiary slot (3rd level).

### Action Cost to Cast

The number of actions required to cast a spell depends on which tier of slot you use:

- Primary Slot: 2 actions
    
- Secondary Slot: 2 actions
    
- Tertiary Slot: Requires preparation (see below)
    

You must have a slot of the appropriate tier for the spell’s level. For instance, a 3rd‑level spell always requires a tertiary slot.

### Casting a Tertiary Spell

To cast a spell using a tertiary slot, you must follow this process:

1. On your turn, spend 1 action to begin charging the spell. This does not expend the slot yet.
    
2. At the start of your next turn, you may spend 2 actions to complete the casting and expend the slot. If you do not, the charge is lost.
    
3. While charging, you must maintain concentration. Any time you take damage before the spell is cast, make a concentration check (using your spellcasting ability) as normal.
    
4. You can hold the charge for a number of turns equal to your mental ability modifier (the one you use for spellcasting). Each turn you hold it, the spell is upcast by one level (to a maximum of 9th level). When you finally cast it, it uses the increased level.
    

## Martial Feats

Martial characters (and half‑casters) gain access to a system of martial feats, special techniques they can activate using feat slots, which refresh on a short rest.

### Feat Slots

Most martials have the following progression of feat slots:

|   |   |
|---|---|
|Level|Slots|
|1–4|3|
|5–10|4|
|11–16|5|
|17–19|6|
|20|7|

Fighters gain one additional slot at each tier (4,5,6,7,8).

### Feats Known

The number of feats a martial knows increases as they level:

- Fighters: 6 + half their level
    
- Most martials: 4 + half their level
    
- Monks: 3 + one‑third their level
    

Feats are divided into four tiers, unlocked at levels 1, 5, 11, and 17. You can learn feats from any tier you have access to.

### Using a Feat

- Activating a feat usually costs 1 or 2 actions (or a reaction) and may require an attack roll or saving throw.
    
- You cannot activate more than one feat on your turn. Reactions used on other turns do not count toward this limit.
    
- The DC for a feat is either:
    
    - Physical DC: 8 + proficiency bonus + Physical Modifier
        
    - Mental DC: 8 + proficiency bonus + Mental Modifier
        

Feat slots are regained after a short rest.
