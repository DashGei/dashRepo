# Races and people of the world

The planet of Gaia is a diverse one, and since the gods arrived to the world they created a plethora of intelligent humanoids to fill its corners. 

When choosing a race, it is recommended to pick one which highlights important parts of your character's traits. For example your big hearted and larger than life Paladin of Sol may be significantly enhanced if you choose to make them an Orc. Your practical naturalist may have a much more graspable character if they are a Moor Elf or Human, leading to meaningful interactions with the world.

## Human

Spanning every continent, humans are the most diverse and ancient of all mortals.
Humankind first evolved from apes, eventually spreading throughout the world and creating the first glimmers of society.

Being one of the two populations not born of gods, humans typically value self determination, resourcefulness, and the natural world. In fact while humans make up about 65% of all surface dwellers, they're only roughly 30% of city dwellers, preferring to stick to hunter gatherer communities and small townships. 

#### Human Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 5'6" – 6'0")

**Age**: Humans typically live 50-70 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Resourceful**: Increase any ability score by 1, gain proficiency in one skill which uses said ability score.

**Primordial**: You can communicate simple ideas to any Beast, or Elemental. It can understand your words, though you have no special ability to understand it in return.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

Many humans do not believe in fate.

Many humans wear face and body paints to connect themselves with the Primordial titans.

Some humans may despise their natural origins and seek religious rebirth.

## Halfling

Primarily residing in the west of their home continent Dvalum, Halflings tend to their gardens and hearths. Stories are spun of their unfathomable ability to remain content and happy with the simple pleasures of life.

Halflings are rarely known to take up adventuring, so they may expect the odd glance from other travelers. Despite these stigmas Halflings can be extraordinarily capable, their small frame giving them the ability to disappear whenever they please, and their patron god Tymora is known to give them a helping hand whenever things get tricky.

#### Halfling Traits

**Size**: Small (about 3'3" – 4'6")

**Age**: Halflings typically live 60-80 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Naturally Stealthy**: You can take the hide action when within 5 feet of another creature who is larger than you.

**Luck**: When you roll a 1 on the d20 of a D20 Test, you can reroll the die, and you must use the new roll.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

The greatest sin a Halfling can commit is that of being impolite.
## Dwarf

Many scholars debate the location of the long lost Dwarvish homeland, whether it resides in the far northern tundras of Pol, in the heart of the great Joursk mountains, or somewhere deep in the howling underdarks of the world. One thing that is known for certain is that a Dwarf's love for good stories, liquor, and a well balanced axe is second to none.

Squat and rosy cheeked, Dwarves are incredibly resilient, and their cultures value quality of craft and character equally. Molded by Hephaestus from the stones of the world, a Dwarf has an uncanny ability to find their way in caves.

#### Dwarf Traits

**Size**: Small (about 4'0" – 5'3")

**Age**: Dwarves typically live 60-80 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Dwarven Resilience**: You have Resistance to Poison damage. You also have Advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the Poisoned condition. 

**Stonecunning**: When standing on a stone surface, you have[[Exploration#Vision | Darkvision]] out to a range of 120 feet, and[[Exploration#Vision | Tremorsense]] out to a range of 10 feet.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

Mountain Dwarves, both male and female alike, are known for their extravagant beards.

The typical greeting between Dwarves of two different houses includes an exchange and inspection of weaponry.

## Orc

Few mortals can hold a candle to the raw power of an Orc who truly believes in what they're fighting for. Orcs can often be found in important societal positions, where they put their skills as natural and captivating leaders to use.

Hale, grey skinned with piercing yellow eyes, Orcs were created by the first sun god Sol, who blessed his children with honorable fervor. Orcs are often deeply passionate and defensive, and while their origins stem from a virtuous god,  Orcs can be deeply aligned with any ideals.

#### Orc Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 5'6" - 6'3")

**Age**: Orcs typically live 50-70 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Radiant Resilience**: You have resistance to Radiant damage. 

**Purge the Unholy**: When you deal damage to an undead creature, you can change the damage type of the attack to radiant.

**Sun Child**: When you are in the light of Sol on the start of your turn, your speed increases by 10 feet. Taking a short rest when in the light of Sol grants you an amount of temporary hit points equal to PB x 2. When these hit points are lost, up to two creatures of your choice within 5 feet of you take Radiant damage equal to your level.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

Tattoos are very important to Orcish society, most of which display scenes of dawn or Sol's animal avatar the boar.

Some of the oldest legends tell tales of the two sun Deities Sol and Ea'or, and many of these stories have been adapted to feature heroic tales of Orcish knights saving delicate Elves.
## Elf

Elves bear pointed ears along with a penchant for fine wine and arts. Many Elves can be described as shrewd or astute folk, who's dedication to mastery of a given skill is palpable. Despite their somewhat cold veneer, Elves are known to be quite poetic and expressive in the right company.

Their creator and second sun deity Ea'or holds domain over the ideas of passion and aesthetics, and these traits are pillars of Elven society. While this indulgence has created a tight-knit culture of philosophers and savants, a notable amount of Elves harbor xenophobic tendencies when interacting with cultures they view as less artistic.

#### Elf Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 6'0 - 6'6")

**Age**: Elves can live up to 200 years, but most die around the ages of 90 - 120

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Silver Sight**: When in moonlight, you have[[Exploration#Vision | Darkvision]] out to 1 mile, and advantage on attack rolls. Additionally when you take a long rest in direct moonlight, you progress 4 hours of the rest for every hour that passes.

**Dedicated Practice**: You gain proficiency with a chosen tool or skill, if you already have proficiency with said choice you gain expertise.

**Subrace**: The Elves of the world come from different backgrounds, and overtime they have adapted to their surroundings, pick one subrace which aligns with your Elf's origins, you gain the features of that subrace.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

Some of the oldest legends tell tales of the two sun Deities Sol and Ea'or, and many of these stories have been adapted to feature heroic tales of Orcish knights saving delicate Elves. 

Elvish is a unique language in that it does not feature hard consonants such as B, G, T, K, D, or P. Elves sometimes have trouble pronouncing words with these sounds.

Male and Female Elves are typically the same height. This extends further with High Elves who to most, sexual dimorphism is entirely imperceptible.

At night Ea'or's moon daughter Lumiere reflects their light so that Elves may see in the dark.

In traditional Elvish culture, at the age of 20 an Elf is to  practice a chosen discipline for one decade, after which they are considered an adult.

Dreams are the most important thing in the world to Deep Elves, and they are habitual journalists in an effort to unlock what may be the secret to the foundations of the multiverse.

### Subraces

**High Elf**: High Elves have dark straight hair and bright orange eyes, hailing from the dunes of the Sym of which their nation shares the same name. The Sym's new leader Serale has declared herself god empress and seeks to control the whole of the the continent by ousting or decimation of all other races. Your High Elf may or may not share the same ideals.

**High Elf Magic**: At level 1 you know the Prestidigitation Cantrip, and at level 5 you learn the Moonbeam spell, it is always prepared and you can cast it once per long rest without expending a spell slot.

**Moor Elf**: Moor Elves are characterized by their striking purple eyes, blonde hair, and  connection to the grounded aspects of the world. To many Moor Elves, physical practices such as archery, pathfinding, textiles, and horseback are their own artistic disciplines. 
Most Moor Elves live on the outskirts of suburbs in the northern half of the continent Y'Valm.

**Moor Elf Pioneering**:  At level 1 your base speed increases by 10 feet. At level 5 you learn one single Tier 1 or 2 Feat with the Exploration tag. Once per Short Rest you can use that Feat without expending a Feat Slot.

**Sea Elf**: Sea elves live in the oceans of the world along with other aquatic races, generally more curious and free spirited in comparison with their land dwelling cousins, Sea Elves have blue skin with black hair and freckles with lively green eyes.

**Children of the Sea**: As a sea elf you have a swimming speed equal to your walking speed +5 feet and the ability to breathe water along with air. Additionally you can communicate simple ideas to any Beast that has a swimming speed. It can understand your words, though you have no special ability to understand it in return.

**Deep Elf**: The most uncommon of all the Elves, Deep Elves almost exclusively stick to their home city of Vorthaliss which resides in the deepest trenches of the South Joursk Ocean. Few mortals know of their existence, but the few scholars who dedicate their lives to studying them pin their society as one intertwined with necromantic practices and extraplanar studies that bridge the gap between insanity and the very basis of sentience.
Deep Elves have dark cyan skin marked with luminescent rings and framed by silver hair. 

**Abyssal Existence**: You have a swimming speed equal to your walking speed and can breathe water, you can hold your breath above ground for a number of hours equal to your PB.  You have[[Exploration#Vision | Darkvision]] out to 60 feet at all times and gain proficiency in stealth. You have advantage on saving throws against spells of the Necromancy school.

## Gnome

Gnomes are a curious group of people, mostly known by the other folk of the world by the various seals stamped into equipment produced and designed by them. The most praised member of any Gnomish family is whoever develops the most efficient and successful invention, and these patents are guarded as a Red Dragon might with their gold.

Folk who have never met a Gnome may be surprised to be greeted by a short statured individual with an unparalleled zest for life. Many famous and infamous companies and businesses are headed by Gnomes, spanning large swaths of continents.

#### Gnome Traits

**Size**: Small (about 3'3" – 4'6")

**Age**: Gnomes typically live 80-90 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Eyes for Quality**: You have Advantage on Ability Checks and Saving Throws against spells of the Illusion school. You also have proficiency in any item created by a Gnome.

**Studied**: You know an additional language and gain proficiency in one Intelligence skill along with two tools or instruments of your choice.

## Tiefling

Tieflings are an incredibly diverse set of mortals who can trace their lineage back generations to the beings of the fiendish planes. All Tieflings share the physical characteristics of their ancestors, and you may want to choose an ancestry which aligns with your Tiefling's personality so make sure to check the Subrace section.

#### Tiefling Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 5'6" – 6'0")

**Age**: Tieflings typically live 50-70 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Retribution**: You always have the spell Hellish Rebuke prepared, and the damage type is the same as the resistance granted to you from your subrace. You may cast this spell once per long rest without spending a spell slot.
#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

### Subraces

**Infernal**: Descendants of Devils share their predecessor's short horns, speared tails, hued skin, and tendency towards more lawful and by the books behavior.  

**Infernal Pact**:  You have resistance to Fire or Lighting damage, and know the Shocking Grasp or Firebolt cantrips.

**Malefic**: Each Mael is a unique entity representing a phobia, formed by the ancient god of fear Ur. Tieflings with Malefic heritage share a deep seated phobia which stems from their ancestor.  Malefic Tieflings are generally self centered and paranoid, and tend to trust their own instincts over others.

**Malefic Magics**:You have resistance to Psychic damage, and you know the Thaumaturgy cantrip.

**Demonic**: Demons are chaotic and ruthless beings and the arch enemies of Devils. Demonic Tieflings share some physical characteristics with Demons, often with thick sideburns, goat horns, and square pupils.

**Demonic Casting**: You have resistance to Poison damage, and you know the Poison Spray cantrip.

## Dragonborn

Dragonborn are strong humanoids which look like wingless, tailless, bipedal dragons. They are known to have personalities in line with their full Dragon ancestors, so if you plan on playing a Dragonborn, make sure to read up on draconic personalities to select one which best fits your character.

Dragonborn are placed intermittently on the continent of Dvalum, but the majority come from and live on the southern continent of Dris where they live alongside their full-blooded ancestors.

#### Dragonborn Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 5'9" - 6'3")

**Age**: Dragonborn typically live 60-80 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Draconic Ancestry**: Your lineage stems from a dragon progenitor. Choose the kind of dragon from the Draconic Ancestors table. Your choice affects your Breath Weapon and Damage Resistance traits as well as your appearance.

| Blue   | Lightning   |
| Brass  | Fire        |
| Bronze | Lightning   |
| Copper | Acid        |
| Gold   | Fire        |
| Green  | Poison      |
| Red    | Fire        |
| Silver | Cold        |

**Breath Weapon**: As an action you exhale a 30-foot Line of magical energy that is 5 feet wide. Each creature in that area must make a Physical DC Dexterity save. On a failed save, a creature takes PB x 1d6 damage of the type determined by your Draconic Ancestry trait. On a successful save, a creature takes half as much damage. You can use this feature once per short rest.

**Damage Resistance**: You have Resistance to the damage type determined by your Draconic Ancestry trait.

**Aquatic Adaptations**: Silver, White, Black, Green, Bronze, and Blue Dragonborn can breathe underwater.

## Goliath

Goliaths are a group of primarily hunter gather and farming people who live on the foothills of the Joursk Mountains. Their massive frames can be quite imposing to onlookers, but they are typically very kind and good natured people.

Goliath society reveres the various ruminants they look after, and Goliaths are usually adorned with sheep tattoos or goat imagery on their clothes. Often, Goliaths are sought out in times where great defenders are needed.

#### Goliath Traits

**Size**: Large (about 6'9" - 8'0")

**Age**: Goliaths typically live 50-70 years

**Speed**: 35 feet walking

**Powerful Build**: Carrying another creature does not reduce your speed as long as it is not larger than you. When you take the Dash action, you ignore difficult terrain when using the speed you gained

**Herder**: You have advantage on Animal Handling checks made with herding creatures such as goats, sheep, and alpacas.

**Long Arm**: All weapons have the thrown property for you. The range for all weapons when thrown is 60/120, and if their damage die is lower than 1d8, it becomes 1d8.

## Tabaxi

Tabaxi are an interesting collection of people, most notably characterized by their feline appearances and behavior. Scattered in three clades across Y'Valm, Tabaxi are known for their reclusive behavior and unreadable expressions.

Their reclusiveness is less because of social customs, and more due to their nocturnal tendencies. Tabaxi in cities often have their own sub towns where other folk gather if they seek more lively nights. Cleanliness, and self image are incredibly important to Tabaxi culture, and many Tabaxi view disrespect of character to be considerably more insulting than to other races.

#### Tabaxi Traits

**Size**: Check Subraces section

**Age**: Tabaxi typically live 50-60 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking and climbing

**Power Nap**: A long rest for you takes 12 hours, but you can split a long rest into multiple 2 hour long segments which may overlap with short rests.

**Night Life**: You have[[Exploration#Vision | Darkvision]] out to 60 feet and gain proficiency in Stealth, Acrobatics,  or Perception checks. You have advantage on Animal Handling checks with feline creatures.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

Tabaxi rarely eat anything except for meat.

### Subraces

**Pantherine**: Pantherine Tabaxi make their homes in the jungles and swamps of western Y'Valm. Their powerful builds allow them to traverse the more difficult terrain and compliment their stern attitudes.

**Pantherine Tabaxi Traits**: You gain proficiency in Athletics and when a creature is grappled by you, you may deal PB x 1d2 Physical damage to it.

**Abyssinian**: Hailing from the deserts of the Sym, Abyssinian Tabaxi have very short hairs as to appear almost as smooth as skin. They are known to be quite resourceful and they traverse the hardships of their hostile environments and neighbors. Abyssinian Tabaxi are typically more aligned with the Primordial titans than any godly beings.

**Abyssinian Tabaxi Traits**: Size: Medium (about 5'9" - 6'3"). You have Advantage on Ability Checks and Saving Throws against spells of the Enchantment and Illusion schools.

**House**: House Tabaxi are short statured and come in a variety of coats. Their diminutive classification and size does them quite the injustice, and they are characterized as being quite lively and curious. Most House Tabaxi come from the northern areas in Y'Valm.

**House Tabaxi Traits**: Size: Small (about 3'6" – 5'0"). Your friendly appearance means that you never have disadvantage on Charisma checks because of hostility and you gain proficiency in one Charisma skill.

## Firbolg

Firbolg are slim, tall folk, with short noses and short grey fur covering their bodies. Lots of communities make sure to keep in touch with nearby families of Firbolg as they have a long and deep tradition of medicinal practices.

Equally sage-like and enigmatic, Firbolg are sometimes approached with caution as their Feywild origins give them otherworldly presences. Many Firbolg are naturalists at heart, and spend their time cultivating teas and caring for wildlife.

#### Firbolg Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 5'9" - 6'9")

**Age**: Firbolg typically live 90-120 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Fey Aspect**: If you know the full and true birth name of any creature, you have advantage on Insight and Persuasion checks against it. You know the Thaumaturgy cantrip and one cantrip from the Druid spell list, when you finish a long rest you can swap that cantrip out with another from the Druid spell list.

**Wood Walker**: You gain proficiency in the Animal Handling or Medicine skills.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

## Genasi

Built in ancient times by the primordial titans, the Genasi were created to wage war and defend Gaia from the interference of the gods. After the primordials lost and were banished to their respective planes, Genasi were left to themselves without a direct purpose.

The appearances of Genasi depend heavily on their ancestral element, those being fire, air water, and earth. Check the subrace section for more details.

#### Genasi Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 5'3" - 6'0")

**Age**: Genasi typically live 50-70 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Elementalist**: You know the Elementalism cantrip.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

### Subraces

**Air Genasi**: Air Genasi have light grey skin, cloudy eyes, and curly, springy hair. As an Air Genasi you have resistance to Lightning damage, and at level 5 you learn the Shocking Grasp cantrip. When you take falling damage, d4s are rolled instead of d6s

**Earth Genasi**: Earth Genasi have olive green skin and flat faces with thick brown hair. As an Earth Genasi you have resistance to Poison damage, and at level 5 you learn the Thorn Whip cantrip. You can communicate simple ideas to any Beast, or Elemental. It can understand your words, though you have no special ability to understand it in return.

**Fire Genasi**: Fire Genasi have skin tones in various ash tones along with bright orange eyes and hair that dances without wind. As an Fire Genasi you have resistance to Fire damage, and at level 5 you learn the Firebolt cantrip. You have[[Exploration#Vision | Darkvision]]  out to 60 feet.

**Water Genasi**: Water Genasi have cyan skin, solid blue eyes, and smooth, bouncy hair. As a Water Genasi you have resistance to Cold damage, and at level 5 you learn the Ray of Frost cantrip. You can also breathe in water along with air and you have a swim speed equal to your walking speed. You can communicate simple ideas to any aquatic Beast, or Elemental. It can understand your words, though you have no special ability to understand it in return.

## Triton

A Triton is characterized by their stocky frame, light scales across the body, and neck gills. Tritons evolved on the extradimensional plane of water, but they make up a large portion of aquatic and even occasionally surface society.

Most Tritons are strong headed and stubborn. Their connection to the primeval world means they rarely bother with political careers, preferring the company of a mill or plowed field. Tales of old describe how Tritons bested great aquatic threats like Krakens or Serpents, and many long to make a legend of their people again.

#### Triton Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 5'6" - 5'9")

**Age**: Tritons typically live 80-100 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking, swimming

**Aquatic Adaptation**: You can breathe air along with water. You have resistance to Cold and Thunder damage. You know the Thunderclap cantrip.

**Primordial**: You can communicate simple ideas to any Beast, or Elemental. It can understand your words, though you have no special ability to understand it in return.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

## Sahuagin

Created by their patron goddess Bellona, Sahuagin are weighty, shark-headed humanoids who are prone to outbursts when they smell blood. Often looked down upon by other members of aquatic society, Sahuagin like to keep a plan to get out of bad spots up their sleeves.

Sahuagin bodies are built to withstand harsher waters like those of the northern reaches in Rimewiel or the smoky crags of Kaori. Despite their general reputation, Sahuagin are extraordinarily well respected trackers and hunters, and many raise sharks as both pets and loyal friends.

#### Sahuagin Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 5'9" - 6'6") or Small (about 3'6" - 4'9")

**Age**: Sahuagin typically live 50-70 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking, swimming

**Aquatic Adaptation**: You can breathe air along with water.

**Blood Hunters**: You have advantage on checks which rely on smell to track creatures below their hitpoint maximum. Additionally when you start your turn you gain PB temporary hitpoints for each creature at 0 hitpoints within 10 feet of you.

**Shark Bond**: You can communicate simple ideas to any Shark. It can understand your words, though you have no special ability to understand it in return.

# Cursed Peoples

In the time the gods have spent looking over the mortals of the world, many groups have fallen out of favor and some have sinned so deeply against their gods as to be cursed for generations to come. For many of these races it may be harder to explain why your character is friendly to their party members, or they may have difficulty interacting meaningfully with others.

## Drow

Of all the civilizations on Gaia, none is more feared than that of the Drow. Those who die defending villages from their raids are often considered the lucky ones, as Drow are known slavers and sadists. Lolth gave her followers dark purple skin, silver hair, and bright red eyes to strike fear in the hearts of their victims.

Long ago when an Elvish enclave came to the middle forests of Dvalum, they forsook their patron deity Ea'or in favor of the malefic spider queen Lolth. Now residing in the deep caverns of the Underdark, Drow stalk the narrow corridors and wait until the moonlight has dimmed to ambush surface dwellers.

#### Drow Traits

**Size**: Medium (about 6'0 - 6'6")

**Age**: Drow can live up to 200 years, but most die around the ages of 90 - 120

**Speed**: 30 feet walking and climbing

**Arachnid Aspects**: You have Resistance to Poison damage. You also have Advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the Poisoned condition. You can communicate simple ideas to any Spider. It can understand your words, though you have no special ability to understand it in return.

With two actions you can make an Unarmed Strike and bite a creature. A bit creature takes 1d4 Physical damage, PB x 1d4 Poison damage, and must make a Physical DC Strength save or be Poisoned for a minute. If the creature is still Poisoned at the end of this minute the creature repeats the saving throw, becoming Paralyzed for an hour if they fail.

**Gloom Stalker**: When not in moon or sunlight, you have[[Exploration#Vision | Darkvision]] out to 1 mile.

#### Optional Character Traits and Trivia

Despite their Elvish origins and appearances, many consider Drow to be more closely related to Malefic Tieflings due to their relationship with Lolth the great Mael of arachnophobia.

## Goblin

Long ago when Gnomes were beginning their societies. A Gnomish king made a deal with the great arch-hag of they Feywilds. This  deal was to give him otherworldly intelligence and inventive ability. When the patron god of Gnomes Hephaestus got wind of this, he became furious that his creations would dare to use anything other than their own ingenuity and determination to excel in his domain. Because of this he cursed the Gnome king and his kingdom to forever be savage, primal, and crude creatures now known as Goblins.

Goblin society typically takes the shape of a clan with a bugbear doing most of the thinking and delegation. Other mutated Goblinoids such as Ogres and Hobgoblins lead raids on villages and towns to plunder food and slaves.

#### Gnome Traits

**Size**: Small (about 3'3" – 4'6")

**Age**: Goblins typically live 30-50 years

**Speed**: 30 feet walking

**Nimble Fingers**: When you take the Disengage or Hide action, your speed increases by 5 feet. additionally you gain proficiency in Stealth or Sleight of Hand checks.

**Dirty Fighting**: When you damage a target who is prone or grappled by another creature, you roll PB x 1d2 and add it to the damage of the attack.
