**For many in the world of Gaia**, Religion plays a communal and utilitarian role in one's life. Rarely will individuals devote themselves to singular gods, or swear all of them off outright.

A typical peasant may pray to Frey in the morning for a safe crop, evoke Ilxi's name while gambling with their friends, and then go home where an emblem of Helm sits above the door and guards against evils.

Just like everyone else, players can form relationships with the various gods of the world, gaining boons, or possibly minor woes if they repeatedly offend some deities.

### Divine Favor

Just like everyone else, players can form relationships with the various gods of the world, gaining boons, or possibly minor woes if they repeatedly offend some deities.

Not every god preferers the same method of worship. Some gods wish only for larger and larger masses of dedicated worshippers, some simply want their follower's to practice their disciplines, and others care solely that their ideals are spread. 

The party loses or gains **Favor Points**  by supporting a deity's efforts and ideals as detailed in each deity's Favor Criteria section, at the DM's discretion unmentioned actions that the party takes may effect relationships with the gods.

Favor grants the entire party small, thematic blessings. Acting against a god’s principles can bring minor woes. A party who makes meaningful connections with each other and the world will always bring favor.

When the party finishes a long rest together, they may ask the DM to reveal their current standings with the gods. They may also choose to voluntarily decrease their Favor with any deity by any amount at that time to make room for other relationships.

### Favor Scale

A party’s standing with a deity ranges from –5 to +10.

| Favor Score | Status     | Effect      |
| ----------- | ---------- | ----------- |
| –5          | Disfavored | Greater Woe |
| –4 to –-3   | Displeased | Minor Woe   |
| 0           | Neutral    | —           |
| 1 – 2       | Noticed    | —           |
| 3 – 5       | Favored    | Minor Boon  |
| 6 – 9       | Honored    | Boon        |
| 10          | Champion   | Major Boon  |

Favor Points adjust at the end of a session at the DM’s discretion. The party cannot lose or gain more than 5 FP per session with a given god through direct offense, and minor slights may not change the score at all.


The party can hold a maximum of 30 Favor Points invested across a maximum of 5  deities at any time. Negative Favor Points do not add to this total, and the party can have a maximum of 10 total negative Favor Points invested. If the party would gain FP that would push their total above 30, those points are lost.

### Worship Methods

Every deity has a **Worship Method** that dictates how the party gains Favor. The DM awards 1 FP when the party meaningfully fulfills the method, and occasionally 2 FP for exceptional acts.

| Method         | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Devotion**   | The god grows stronger through worship. FP is earned by spreading the faith, building shrines, converting NPCs, or leading communal prayers.                                                                            |
| **Discipline** | The god values the practice of a craft, skill, or way of life. FP is earned by engaging in the associated discipline. Creating art, mastering a trade, or upholding a code may be the best way to earn this gods favor. |
| **Ideals**     | The god cares that their ideals are enacted. FP is earned by championing those ideals, defeating opposing forces, or shifting a community’s values.                                                                     |

### Boons & Woes

Boons are occasional minor bonuses that the party may obtain by maintaining good relationships with gods. Each god has their own set of boons, some may give players resources to spend during the day, some may only activate when the DM chooses.

## Ea’or and Lumiere

_The Second Sun, Patron of Passion and Aesthetics_
_The Pale Moon, Patron of Archery and Discipline_

Ea'or  is the embodiment of the smaller, fuchsia star in the center of the planescape. Ea'or was created alongside their companion god Sol when the central star was split upon arrival of the gods. Ea'or is responsible for the creation of Elves, and their daughter goddess Lumiere who embodies the moon.

**Domains:** Beauty, dedication, passion, Elves
**Worship Method:** Discipline (creating or appreciating art, perfecting a craft)

**Favor Criteria:**

- The party commissions, restores, or protects a significant work of art.
    
- A party member spends meaningful downtime practicing a craft and produces something of quality.
    
- Two or more party members share a bottle of wine in Ea’or’s name.
    
- The party thwarts a Drow or agent(s) of Lolth.

**Disfavor Criteria:**

- The party deliberately destroys an artwork.
    
- They mock or dismiss passionate expression as foolish.
    
- They side with forces that suppress artistic freedom.
    
- The party sides with Drow or agent(s) of Lolth.

**Boons:**

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Silver Sight.** One per Long rest the DM bestows the party with the Silver Sight racial bonus for one hour. An Elf receives the benefits even if they are not in moonlight.
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): Lumiere’s Grace.** Once per week (resetting on Same'ih), the party can complete a long rest in 4 hours instead of 8 if bathed in direct moonlight. Additionally, any Charisma ability checks made on the subsequent day gain a +1 bonus.
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): Ea’or’s Muse.** Once per long rest, when a party member fails a skill check related to art, performance, or persuasion through beauty, the DM can choose to add 2d10 to the roll. The success is accompanied by a subtle divine sign (a shaft of moonlight, a faint melody).


**Woes:**

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4): Jaded Eye.** The first Charisma (Performance) check a party member makes per day suffers a –1d4 penalty.
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5): Fading Light.** Moonlight seems dimmer to the party. During nighttime, they have disadvantage on Perception checks that rely on sight unless they use artificial light.

## Frey
_The Harvest Lord, Guardian of the Hearth_

Frey is the patron god of Halflings, homely ideals, and taking in strangers from the night. It isn't difficult to find signs of Frey worship and his (or sometimes her) followers are known to leave kind hearted messages above fireplaces or on the beds of inns they especially enjoyed. Many tavern-esque establishments leave offering tables to Frey in the middle of their main rooms.

**Domains:** Community, fertility, hospitality, Halflings
**Worship Method:** Devotion (building and maintaining communities, leading harvest rites)

**Favor Criteria:**

- They defend a farmstead or common folk from a threat.
    
- The party partakes in a communal meal or celebration in Frey’s name.
    
- The party invites one or more NPCs over for dinner.
    
- They convince others to honor Frey’s simple rituals.

**Disfavor Criteria:**

- They abandon a community in need when aid was within their power.
    
- They mock the value of hard, honest labor.
    
- The party is exceptionally impolite to people hosting them.

**Boons:**

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Hearty Meal.** Once per long rest, when the party finishes a short rest and consumes food, each member regains an extra 1d4 hit points.
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): Kind Beginnings.** Once per long rest, when the party meets a new NPC, 1d10 is added to the first Charisma check made with that creature.
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): Fields of Plenty.** Once per week, when a party member opens an unoccupied room (such as a rented chamber, an abandoned cottage, or a cleared cellar), the DM may have a _Heroes’ Feast_ spell appear inside, laid out moments before their arrival. A card depicting a cornucopia with fine lettering reading, _“To my good friends, come and make merry,”_ rests plainly upon the feast.

**Woes:**

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4):** Once per week, when the party visits a hostel or inn, that inn is closed or otherwise unaccepting of guests.
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5):** Once per week at the DM's discretion, parties must make a DC 15 Strength saving throw or be poisoned by bad food for an hour upon waking.


## Sol

_The First Sun, the Orc-Father, Patron of Valor and Sacrifice_

Sol is the greater, golden star that warms the world and the patron god of Orcs. He appears as a towering male Orc in gleaming, gilded plate, or as a colossal boar whose tusks sweep low and wide. His followers champion righteousness through action, not words, and believe that the truest good is a life laid down for others.

**Domains:** Righteousness, valor, self‑sacrifice, dawn, Orcs  
**Worship Method:** Ideals (championing good through bravery and self‑sacrifice)

### Favor Criteria

- A party member takes a significant risk to protect an innocent or ally.
    
- The party defeats a powerful evil without resorting to dishonorable tactics.
    
- Two or more party members swear an oath together before a battle or important undertaking.
    
- The party defeats or thwarts an undead or fiendish creature.

### Disfavor Criteria

- The party flee a winnable fight..
    
- They break a sworn oath or promise made in good faith.
    
- A party member sacrifices another for personal gain or cowardice.

### Boons

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Sol’s Vigor.** Once per long rest, when a party member drops below half hitpoints, the spell Heroism is cast on them using that character's Mental Modifier
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): Sol’s Resolve.** Once per long rest, when a party member fails a saving throw against the frightened or charmed condition, the DM may allow them to succeed instead. If the condition was caused by an undead or fiend, the source takes radiant damage equal to your level.
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): Bathed in Light.** Once per long rest, one party member can cast one of the spells Daylight, Spirit Guardians, or if they have direct sunlight, Flame Strike  without the expenditure of a spell slot. 

### Woes

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4): Waning Courage.** Once per day, the first time one party member would gain the frightened condition, all party members within 15 feet of them must also make the same saving throw or suffer the full effects.
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5): Shunned by the Light.** While in direct sunlight, the party no longer benefits from healing during short rests. Additionally, undead, celestials, and fiendish creatures have advantage on attacks against them until dusk.

## Lucifer

_The Man in Chains, Patron of Half‑Truths and Comeuppance_

Any and all evil creatures have made a pact with Lucifer whether they know it or not. The pact dictates eternal servitude and devotion,  in lieu of eternal damnation. The majority of Luciferic worshipers are tyrants and debt collectors, but some cults devoted to Lucifer believe they may be reborn  as cambions if they dedicate their lives to him.

**Domains:** Half‑truths, betrayal, debts, comeuppance, Hell, Infernal Tieflings  
**Worship Method:** Ideals (making deals and punishing those who break them)

### Favor Criteria

- The party lies for meaningful gain.
    
- The party successfully collects a debt from someone who thought they had escaped it.
    
- The party wins against a foe by underhanded tactics.
    
- A party member traps an enemy in the exact words of their own promise.
    
- The party turns an enemy’s betrayal back upon them.

### Disfavor Criteria

- A party member tells the truth to an outside influence when that truth directly harms another party member.
    
- The party forgives a significant debt without extracting any payment or punishment.
    
- A party member breaks the letter of a freely made agreement.

### Boons

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Well‑Chosen Words.** Once per long rest, when a party member makes a Deception check, they may add 1d4 to the roll.
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): A Devil’s Due.** Once per long rest, when a party member is reduced to 0 hit points. The creature that damaged them becomes marked with an invisible sigil; the party knows its direction for 1 hour. If the marked creature is reduced to 0 hit points before the hour ends, the party member regains all of their hitpoints.
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): Hellish Arbiter.** Once per week (resetting on Lucae), the party may make a pact: When an NPC willingly signs a written agreement without magical influence a Cambion appears and writes his own name alongside the NPC's. If the NPC ever breaks this agreement they are immediately reduced to 1 hitpoint and teleported to the party's location with the restrained condition for an hour as they are bound by hot iron chains. A creature bound in this way in unable to verbalize anything but truths. This part of the pact must be stated in the agreement.

### Woes

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4): Forked Tongue.** Once per day, when a single party member attempts a Persuasion check, they must subtract 1d4 from the roll.
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5): Mark of the Default.** A faint, infernal brand appears on the party’s palms, visible only to fiends and those who deal in souls. Devils and other lawful evil outsiders know if the party tells a lie to them, and their attacks add an additional 1d4 fire damage to party members.

## Helm

_The First Knight, Patron of Readiness and Overland Storms_

Helm is the god of watchfulness, preparation, and the sudden storm that tests a traveler’s mettle. He appears as an unreadable knight in full gothic plate, visor forever down. He speaks rarely, and only to those who have proven their vigilance. His is known by condensation on cold metal, the low rumble of distant thunder, the silhouette of a falcon against grey skies, and the smell of petrichor before rain.

**Domains:** Watchfulness, preparedness, overland storms, raptors
**Worship Method:** Discipline (the constant practice of vigilance; maintaining one’s gear, mind, and surroundings as if battle could come at any moment)

### Favor Criteria

- The party posts a full watch rotation even when they believe themselves safe.
    
- A party member maintains their equipment during downtime.
    
- The party spots an ambush before it is sprung.
    
- Two or more party members stand watch together and discuss their past failures, vowing to learn from them.

### Disfavor Criteria

- The party is caught completely unaware by an enemy.
    
- The party dismisses a threat as paranoia.
    
- The party becomes lost due to carelessness.

### Boons

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Watchful Eye.** Once per long rest, when a party member makes a Wisdom (Perception) check, they may add 1d4 to the roll..
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): Stormwarden’s Grace.** Once per week, the DM may bestow a moment of supernatural vigilance. When the party would be surprised, a sudden chill washes over them, the surprise is negated, and 1d4 is added to each party member's initiative roll.
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): Helm’s Vigil.** Once per week, the DM may allow the party to enact the Vigil of the Watcher. When the party takes a long rest in a defensible location, they may perform a brief rite: one party member draws the sign of a closed visor in the dirt or traces it on a shield. That night, the area is protected as if by a _Forbiddance_ spell that damages fiends and undead. Additionally, any creature attempting to scry upon the party during this rest must make a Wisdom saving throw; on a failure, they see only a silent knight in full armor staring back. The protection lasts until dawn.

### Woes

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4): Unwatched Moment.** Once per day, the first time a party member makes an ability check to notice a hidden creature, trap, or environmental hazard, they roll with disadvantage.
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5): Storm’s Ire.** Once per week, when the party travels overland, the DM may send a localized storm to harry them. The storm lasts 1 hour, imposing disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight or sound, extinguishing unprotected flames, and halving their travel speed. The party’s first attempt to take a short rest during this storm is interrupted by a sudden, deafening thunderclap.

## Gri

_The Lantern Lord, Patron of  the Harvest, Travel, and the Calm End_

Gri walks the Shadowfell, but his hand touches the harvest fields and the lonely road alike. He is the patron of autumn’s last bounty, the traveler who reaches the inn just as the door closes, and the gentle guide who leads souls into the afterlife. His followers pray for full granaries, safe journeys, and a peaceful death for those they love.

**Domains:** Harvest, autumn to early winter, safe travel, the process of death.  
**Worship Method:** Devotion (communal observance; offering thanks at harvest, praying at roadside shrines, and performing proper funeral rites)

### Favor Criteria

- The party leaves an offering (food, coin, or a lit candle) at a roadside shrine or crossroads.
    
- They aid a community with its harvest, or protect a harvest from ruin.
    
- They ensure a fallen enemy or stranger receives proper burial rites.
    
- Two or more party members share stories of lost loved ones..
    
- The party sends an ancient undead to the afterlife.

### Disfavor Criteria

- The party disturbs a grave, tomb, or funeral without cause.
    
- They destroy a harvest or waste food in abundance.
    
- They abandon a dying companion without attempting to ease their passage.
    
- The party raises or disturbs the dead.

### Boons

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Pathmaker’s Blessing.** Once per long rest, when the party is travelling they can roll 1d6, on a 4-6 the party completes two days of travel in half the time.
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): Lain to Rest.** Once per long rest, one member of the party can use the Cleric Turn Undead feature as an action.
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): The Final Harvest.** On Frejdags, the party gains the benefits of the Death Ward spell when they take a long rest.

### Woes

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4): Stalled Journey.** Once per day, the party’s overland travel is mysteriously hindered: a sudden fog, a washed‑out bridge, or an impassable fallen tree delays them for 1d4 hours.
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5): Chill of the Grave.** Once per week, the party’s long rest is plagued by nightmares of the Shadowfell. They regain only half their hitpoints (rounded down).

## Leviathan

_The Primordial Titan of Water, the Unstoppable Force_

Leviathan is the elemental titan of all waters; from the placid surface of a forest pond to the ship‑cracking fury of a maelstrom. Like other titans, Leviathan does not seek worship nor care for mortal morality. It values only the unbroken cycle of nature: currents that run free, shores unpoisoned, the deep places left undisturbed.

**Domains:** Water, oceans, storms, the natural cycle, balance, the deep  
**Worship Method:** Ideals (defending the integrity of nature, especially waters, and opposing those who exploit, pollute, or unnaturally command the elements)

### Favor Criteria

- The party cleanses a polluted water source or prevents an impending contamination.
    
- They offer a meaningful gift to a body of water before a voyage (a coin, a ration, a drop of their own blood).
    
- They defeat a creature or force that seeks to control harm aquatic environments for gain.

### Disfavor Criteria

- The party pollutes a body of water.
    
- The party hunts aquatic creatures for sport.
    
- They aid a creature or force that seeks to control harm aquatic environments for gain.

### Boons

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Calm Waters.** Once per long rest, the party can will a small body of water (a lake, cove, or river stretch) to become preternaturally calm for 1 hour. Water vehicles within the area gain advantage on all checks to navigate or remain upright, and seasickness is suppressed entirely among the party and their allies.
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): Tidal Surge.** Once per week (resetting on Prydei), the DM may cast the spell Control Water to the party's benefit.
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): Leviathan’s Embrace.** Once long rest a party member can cast the spell Summon Elemental without the use of a spell slot.

### Woes

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4): Unsettled Seas.** Once per day, whenever the party travels on or near water, the water becomes unnaturally choppy. All Dexterity (Acrobatics) checks and concentration saving throws made while on a boat have disadvantage until the party sets foot on dry land.
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5): Maelstrom’s Wrath.** Once per week, one party member who enters a body of water is immediately attacked by 1d4 Water Weirds.

## Kaori

_Primordial Titan of Fire, Phoenix Mother_

Kaori is the elemental titan of fire. In the millennium since the war with the gods, she has been largely dormant. Defeat at the hands of Sol left her wounded not in body but in spirit, and she now sits in her elemental plane in a state of quiet melancholy. Yet she remains a titan, and those who seek rebirth, renewal, or the courage to rise from ash will find her embers still warm.


**Domains:** Fire, rebirth, pyrotechnics, renewal 
**Worship Method:** Ideals (defending nature's right to burn and renew, and aiding those who seek genuine rebirth or transformation)

### Favor Criteria

- The party prevents the unnatural suppression or extinction of a natural fire (a volcanic vent, a sacred flame, an eternal pyre).
    
- They aid a creature or community seeking genuine rebirth or reinvention.
    
- They offer something meaningful to a fire before a journey: a written regret, a lock of hair, a dry branch.
    
- Two or more party members sit vigil together through a long night, speaking of what they wish to leave behind and what they hope to become.

### Disfavor Criteria

- The party extinguishes a natural or sacred flame without cause.
    
- They mock or dismiss someone's sincere attempt to change or begin anew.
    
- They use fire to torture, terrorize, or destroy.

### Boons

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Ember's Warmth.** When the party finishes a short rest near any flame (a campfire, a candle, a torch), each member regains an additional 1d4 hit points. If a party member is suffering from exhaustion, the rest counts as twice the duration for the purpose of reducing exhaustion levels.
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): Phoenix Heart.** When the party finishes a short rest near any flame (a campfire, a candle, a torch), each member regains an additional 1d4 hit points. 
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): Kaori's Rebirth.** Once per long rest, a party member can cast the spell _Fire Shield_ (warm shield only) without expending a spell slot. Additionally, if the shielded creature is reduced to 0 hit points while the spell is active.
    

### Woes

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4): Fading Warmth.** Once per day, the first time each party member would gain the benefits of a short or long rest, any fire within 20 feet gutters and dims. Healing from hit dice during this rest is reduced by 1 per die rolled (minimum 1).
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5): Ash in the Mouth.** Once per week, the party's food and water taste of cold ash. They require double the normal amount of rations to gain the benefits of a short rest, and potions of healing heal only half the normal amount (rounded down) for the next 24 hours. This effect ends at the next dawn.

## Ao

_The Primordial Titan of Air_

Ao is the elemental titan of air—the gentle breeze that fills a child's kite, the roaring gale that strips mountains bare, and every current between. Unlike Leviathan's silent depth or Kaori's wounded stillness, Ao is carefree, curious, and utterly indifferent to mortal consequence. Ao has fleeting memory, and the past actions of the gods bear no power over his actions

**Domains:** Air, wind, sky, sound, freedom, caprice  
**Worship Method:** Ideals (defending the open sky from those who would chain it, and celebrating freedom in all its forms)

### Favor Criteria

- The party destroys or disables a device or spell designed to control the weather or bind air elementals.
    
- They free a flying creature from captivity.
    
- The party plays a harmless prank.
    
- The party defeats a creature that imprisons others.

### Disfavor Criteria

- The party is cruel to or harms a flying beast.
    
- They aid in the construction or maintenance of a structure that magically suppresses weather or natural wind.
    
- They mock the idea of freedom or call the carefree foolish.
    
- The party clips the wings of a captured enemy, literal or metaphorical, when they could have shown mercy.

### Boons

- **Minor Boon (FP 3–5): Tailwind.** When a party member, ally, or object falls from a height tall enough to deal fall damage, the DM rolls 1d6, on a 2-6 the spell Feather Fall is cast on the target.
    
- **Boon (FP 6–9): Whisper on the Wind.** Once per week (resetting on Prydei), the DM may allow the party to send a message as if by the _Sending_ spell, carried on a gust of wind to any creature they have met. The reply arrives within 1 minute as a soft breeze that speaks directly into the recipient's ear. Additionally, the DM may use this boon to deliver a cryptic hint or rumor to the party from Ao's rambling consciousness.
    
- **Major Boon (FP 10): Unchained Ascent.** Once per long rest, a party member can cast the spell _Fly_ on up to three creatures without expending a spell slot. The flight is accompanied by a constant, playful tailwind that makes cloaks and hair stream dramatically. Additionally, while flying under this effect, a creature can use a reaction to reroll a failed Dexterity saving throw.

### Woes

- **Minor Woe (FP –3 to –4): Contrary Wind.** Once per day, when the party attempts overland travel, an inexplicable headwind rises against them. Their travel speed is halved for 1d4 hours, and ranged weapon attacks made during this time have disadvantage while outside.
    
- **Greater Woe (FP –5): Zephyr's Prank.** Once per week (resetting on Prydei), The DM casts the spells Wind Wall or Control Winds on the party
